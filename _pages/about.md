I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Washington University in St. Louis, advised by Prof. Ning Zhang in the [Computer Security and Privacy Laboratory](https://cybersecurity.seas.wustl.edu/).

My research focuses on trustworthy AI-enabled cyber-physical and agentic systems. I am interested in how AI systems behave when deployed in physical, robotic, and agent-to-agent environments, especially under adversarial or uncertain conditions.

My current work studies physical-world adversarial robustness for object detectors and secure infrastructure for emerging Internet of Agents ecosystems. More broadly, I am interested in robotics and perception security, physical-to-digital attack surfaces, agent discovery, capability attestation, privacy exposure, reputation, and trust establishment in open agent-to-agent systems.

Before joining Washington University in St. Louis, I received a B.S. in Computer Science from Columbia University. I have also worked on serverless scheduling, programming languages, and systems research.
