# Research

My research focuses on trustworthy AI-enabled cyber-physical and agentic systems. I am interested in how AI systems behave when deployed in physical, robotic, and agent-to-agent environments, especially under adversarial or uncertain conditions.

## Current Research

### Physical-World Robustness of Object Detectors

I study physical-world adversarial patch robustness against object detectors, with emphasis on how attacks survive the physical-to-digital pipeline from actuation and environmental transmission to sensing and preprocessing.

This work combines simulation and physical testbeds involving YOLOv5, CARLA, Gazebo, ROS2, PX4/drone setups, real camera capture, and printed adversarial objects and patches. As part of this effort, I developed a Gazebo-based adversarial-example testing environment for evaluating physical adversarial examples in simulated robotic and cyber-physical settings.

### Internet of Agents

I am exploring security challenges in emerging Internet of Agents ecosystems, where LLM agents can publish capabilities, discover other agents, and communicate to complete user tasks.

My current work includes developing an initial lab-scale prototype for agent publication and discovery using an ADS server based on Agntcy A2A/ADS concepts. I am interested in agent certification, privacy exposure, capability attestation, reputation, and trust establishment in open agent-to-agent systems.

## Exploratory Work

### Evaluation of Vision-Language-Action Models on LIBERO

I conducted an exploratory evaluation of instruction-following limitations in vision-language-action models on LIBERO through prompt ablations and scene-manipulation tests. This work is not my main current research direction.
